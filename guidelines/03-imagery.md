# 03 · Imagery & Photography

How Yard Mastery visuals should *look*. This governs both photography selection and AI-generated images. Pair with `prompts/image-prompt-library.md` (ready-to-use prompts) and `prompts/style-tokens.md` (reusable fragments).

---

## 1. The Yard Mastery look in one line
**Bright, healthy, real American lawns — pro results a DIYer can actually achieve.** Lush green turf, warm natural light, clean product shots, and proud homeowners. Aspirational but believable, never sterile or fake.

## 2. Image categories (what we make)

1. **Product shots** — the bag/bottle, clean and heroic. On white (e-comm) or staged on a real lawn / in-use. Show both bag sizes when relevant (18 lb + 45 lb).
2. **Product-in-use / action** — spreading granular fert, spraying with the backpack sprayer, holding the soil test kit. Hands and gear, "I can do this" energy.
3. **Lifestyle / lawn pride** — homeowner enjoying the finished lawn (family, dog, BBQ, bare feet on grass). "Lawns are where memories are made."
4. **Results / proof** — thick green turf close-ups, stripes/mow lines, before→after, "double dark" color.
5. **Seasonal / thematic** — heat & drought (summer), seeding (fall), pre-emergent timing, holiday/Christmas-lights, Memorial Day.
6. **Promo graphics** — sale layouts with price, % off, CTA buttons, product cutouts on color blocks. (Design, not photography — see `05-layout-and-channels.md`.)

## 3. Photography style

- **Lighting:** natural daylight; golden-hour warmth is ideal. Bright and optimistic. Soft shadows. Avoid harsh midday flatness or dim/moody lighting.
- **Color/grade:** vivid but natural greens — turf should look *healthy and saturated*, not radioactive neon or AI-fake. Slightly warm white balance. True-to-life product colors.
- **Setting:** real residential yards, suburban American homes, backyards, fence lines, driveways. Relatable, well-kept but lived-in (not a magazine mansion).
- **Subjects/people:** everyday homeowners — diverse, ages 30–65, casual clothes. Genuine, candid expressions (satisfaction, focus, pride). Hands-on. No stiff stock-photo poses, no business attire.
- **Composition:** clear subject, uncluttered. Strong foreground product or action; lawn/home as supporting context. Leave **clean negative space** for headline + logo (top third or one side). Rule of thirds.
- **Depth:** shallow depth of field for product/action hero shots (subject sharp, lawn softly blurred) reads premium and intentional.
- **Authenticity:** real grass blades, real granules, real water droplets. Believable coverage and results.

## 4. Composition for layouts (leave room to brand it)
Most images become an email/social graphic, so shoot/generate with **overlay space**:
- Keep the top third or one vertical third relatively open for the **headline**.
- Reserve a corner of clean space for the **logo**.
- Keep the main subject off-center when text will sit beside it.
- Generate at the target aspect ratio (see `05-layout-and-channels.md`): 1:1 and 4:5 for social, 16:9 for YouTube/hero, ~2:3 vertical for stories.

## 5. Do / Don't

**Do**
- Show **real, achievable results** — thick, even, green turf.
- Put the **product in context** (on the lawn, in hand, mid-application).
- Use warm, natural light and vivid-but-believable greens.
- Keep it clean with space for text and logo.
- Reflect the season and the grass type (warm vs cool) when relevant.

**Don't**
- No fake/oversaturated "alien green" or plastic-looking lawns.
- No cluttered, busy backgrounds that fight the product.
- No dim, gloomy, or cold-blue grading.
- No stiff corporate stock vibes or obviously staged smiles.
- No competitor products, brand-name big-box bags, or recognizable third-party logos.
- Don't let AI render the logo or important text — composite real assets afterward.
- Avoid weeds/dead patches/pests unless the piece is specifically a "problem→solution" story (then show the fix).

## 6. Recurring motifs & props
Lawn stripes/mow lines · granular fertilizer spreading from a broadcast spreader · backpack/hose-end sprayer in use · soil test kit + soil plug · close-up turf blades with dew · bare feet / dog / kids on grass · suburban home + green front lawn · the YM bag (green/gold) as a hero object · "before & after" splits · American/made-in-USA cues (subtle, never kitschy).

## 7. Brand-fit checklist for any image
- [ ] Lawn/greens look **healthy and real** (not fake-neon)?
- [ ] Lighting warm & bright?
- [ ] Product accurate (right bag color, right size, no garbled text)?
- [ ] Clean space reserved for headline + logo?
- [ ] Correct aspect ratio for the channel?
- [ ] On-season and on-message?
- [ ] No competitor/third-party brands or off-brand clutter?
